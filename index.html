Projects

Print 1

An important part of our 3D printing process was creating the overhang test, which was meant to verify the printer's capacity to handle intricate geometries. At first, the printer's accuracy was hampered by machine mistakes, which posed substantial hurdles. The initial attempt was not very successful since the structure was unstable and the overhangs were not well delineated. Subsequent attempts failed even after recalibrating the equipment and resolving the issues. The overhangs were clearly not up to the design criteria, indicating the printer's inaccuracy and underscoring the need for more improvements in both printing quality and machine dependability..

Print 2

A unique set of difficulties arose throughout the 3D printing process for the jewelry stand, which I had customized in Tinkercad with my pet's name. We had a substantial adhesion error in spite of our meticulous design revisions, which included specific touches to make the stand distinctive. Due to this problem, the print was unable to adhere to the build platform correctly, resulting in an unfinished and misaligned structure. Due to incorrect adhesion, the stand's base warped and detached during the print, resulting in a failed print. This failure brought to light the necessity of improved adhesive solutions and bed preparation in order to guarantee a good and stable print result..

Print 3

The Autodesk Fusion-designed diamond-shaped jewelry holder had serious problems when it was 3D printed. Despite the careful attention to detail that went into making the design both aesthetically beautiful and useful, a crucial structural flaw ultimately caused the design to fail. In particular, it turned out that the prongs meant to secure the jewelry were overly brittle. These prongs broke at the conclusion of the print process as it went on. This breakage not only jeopardized the holder's integrity but also prevented the machine from finishing the remaining sections of the printing job. This mishap highlighted how crucial it is to strengthen fragile elements during the design stage in order to avoid similar problems in subsequent prints.

Print 4

I made the decision to alter my most recent project in order to solve the problems I had run into with earlier 3D printing ventures. Even though the first design was better than the previous iterations, a weak joint point caused it to fail. The print failed partially as a result of this vulnerability. Still, this setback yielded insightful information. By adding organic supports and a 10% increase in the inlay, I was able to further polish the design and greatly improve the structural stability. The updated design printed effectively, exhibiting robustness and dependability. This final version was not only robust, but it also demonstrated the value of iterative design changes and the necessity of strengthening important structural components..
